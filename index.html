📊 Understanding Solar Bills

How to Read Your Solar Bill

Learn to understand net metering, generation credits, and consumption charges

Your solar bill contains several key sections:

1. Generation Section: Shows how much electricity your solar panels produced this month (kWh).

2. Consumption Section: Shows how much electricity you used from the grid.

3. Net Metering Credits: When you produce more than you consume, excess energy is fed back to the grid and you receive credits.

4. Monthly Net Amount: This is your final bill amount after adjusting credits and consumption.

Example: If you generated 300 kWh and consumed 250 kWh from grid, you have 50 kWh credit for next month.

Fixed Charges: Some utility companies charge fixed monthly fees regardless of net metering status.

Net Metering Explained

Understanding how excess solar energy converts to bill credits

Net Metering is a billing mechanism that:

During Day: Your solar panels generate electricity. Excess power flows back to the grid.

During Night: You use electricity from the grid when solar panels aren't generating.

Monthly Settlement: Utility company calculates net consumption vs generation.

Credit System: Excess generation credits are carried forward to next month.

Annual True-up: At year end, any remaining credits are usually paid at wholesale rates.

Bi-directional Meter: Special meter tracks electricity flowing both ways.

Why is My Bill Still Positive?

Common reasons for having a bill even with solar panels

Several factors can cause positive bills:

1. System Size: Your solar system might be smaller than your energy needs.

2. Seasonal Variation: Less sun in monsoon reduces generation.

3. Fixed Charges: Monthly connection charges, meter rent, electricity duty are not covered by net metering.

4. High Consumption: Using more electricity than usual (AC, heaters, new appliances).

5. System Performance: Dust, shading, or technical issues reducing efficiency.

6. Time-of-Use Rates: Different rates for peak/off-peak hours.

⚡ System Performance

Monitoring Your Solar System

Tools and tips to track your solar panel performance

Key Performance Indicators to Monitor:

1. Daily Generation (kWh): Check your inverter display or monitoring app daily.

2. System Efficiency: Compare actual vs expected generation based on system size.

3. Weather Impact: Understand how clouds, rain affect daily output.

4. Seasonal Patterns: Summer generates more, monsoon generates less.

5. Monthly Trends: Track cumulative generation month-over-month.

Monitoring Tools: Most inverters come with mobile apps, web portals, or physical displays.

Warning Signs: Sudden drops in generation, error codes, or unusual patterns need attention.

Seasonal Performance Variations

What to expect throughout the year in Maharashtra

Maharashtra Solar Performance by Season:

Summer (March-May): Best performance - 7-8 hours of peak sun, maximum generation.

Monsoon (June-September): Reduced performance - 3-4 hours peak sun due to clouds.

Winter (November-February): Good performance - 6-7 hours peak sun, cool weather helps efficiency.

Post-Monsoon (October): Excellent performance - clear skies, clean panels.

Expected Variation: 40-60% lower generation in monsoon vs summer is normal.

Annual Pattern: Your system typically generates 60% in summer, 20% in monsoon, 20% in winter.

🔧 Maintenance & Troubleshooting

Regular Maintenance Schedule

Keep your solar system running at peak efficiency

Monthly Maintenance:

• Check generation readings and compare with previous months

• Visual inspection of panels for damage or excessive dirt

• Clean panels if very dusty (especially after dust storms)

Quarterly Maintenance:

• Professional cleaning of panels and inverter ventilation

• Check all electrical connections and cables

• Inspect mounting structure for rust or loose bolts

Annual Maintenance:

• Comprehensive system health check by certified technician

• Inverter performance analysis and software updates

• Safety inspection of earthing and electrical protection

Common Issues & Solutions

Troubleshoot common solar system problems

Low Generation Issues:

Problem: System generating less than expected

Solutions: Clean panels, check for shading, inspect for damage, verify inverter operation

Inverter Not Working:

Problem: No display or error messages

Solutions: Check AC/DC breakers, reset inverter, call technician if problem persists

High Bill Despite Solar:

Problem: Electricity bill higher than expected

Solutions: Check system performance, review energy usage patterns, verify net metering setup

📋 Government Policies

Maharashtra Solar Policy 2025

Latest updates on subsidies, net metering, and regulations

Key Policy Highlights:

Subsidies Available:

• Rs.30,000/Kw subsidy upto 2kw for residential Households

• Rs.18,000/Kw subsidy for additional capacity between 2kw-3kw for residential Households

• For systems greater than 3 kW, the subsidy is capped at ₹ 78,000

Net Metering:

• Available for systems up to 1MW capacity

• Bi-directional meter provided by utility company

• Excess credit carried forward for 12 months

Approvals Required:

• Online application through state portal

• Technical feasibility study for systems >10kW

• Safety clearance from electrical inspector
